# SORKIN-2 first physical boundary: historical Bombelli annealer

## Physical question

Does the historical Bombelli annealer — with its specific energy function, move
set, temperature schedule (T₀=100, γ=0.9), and acceptance rule — recover known-truth
causal configurations, and at what size does it first fail to do so?

All four runs below use `annealer_mode = historical/default`. No tuned schedule,
no guarded warmup. Each case is a distinct family or size; they are not comparable
without qualification.

## Interpretation

**chain_4_d2 and antichain_4_d2** are extreme calibration cases. A total chain and a
total antichain are the maximal and empty causal orders on four events. Both are
guaranteed embeddable by elementary construction; they bound the space of possible
causal orders. Recovering them confirms the apparatus is operative at the trivial
extremes. They are not physical results.

**minkowski_6_s1959_d2** is the first non-trivial physical case. The causal order
is generated by Minkowski sprinkling at seed 1959 in 1+1D; the ground-truth
coordinates are the independent truth source. The annealer recovers all 4 target
causal relations exactly under the historical schedule. This is the first case
where SORKIN-2 asks a genuine recoverability question and the historical annealer
answers affirmatively.

**minkowski_12_s1962_d2_hist** is the first algorithmic failure under historical
schedule. The annealer induces only 1 of 19 target causal relations, with 19
missing and 1 spurious relation, exhausting 35 cooling blocks at final energy
≈ 16.8. The ground-truth realization exists — the sprinkler coordinates at seed
1962 have zero energy by construction — but the historical annealer did not access
it.

**The observed boundary is algorithmic, not ontological.** The transition from
N=6 (exact match) to N=12 (failure) marks the limit of what the historical
Bombelli procedure can access in this comparison, not a statement about whether
the N=12 causal set is embeddable. These are two distinct questions and must not
be conflated.

---

## Cautions

- **No non-embeddability claim.** The zero-energy realization for N=12, seed 1962
  exists by construction. The annealer's failure is evidence about the algorithm,
  not about the causal set.

- **No manifoldlikeness claim.** Neither success nor failure at any of these cases
  is interpretable as manifoldlikeness. The energy function and move set of the
  Bombelli annealer are not manifoldlikeness detectors.

- **No generalization to other seeds.** Each result is specific to its case_id,
  seed, and annealer mode. No statement is made about recovery rates across seeds
  or about what would happen at seed 1959 with N=12 or seed 1962 with N=6.

- **No comparison with tuned or guarded-warmup schedules yet.** The results above
  are all under `historical/default`. Rows D and E of the known-truth matrix define
  a future comparison between historical and tuned schedules for N=12; that
  comparison has not been made.

- **exact_match is the primary recovery observable.** Low final energy is a
  necessary but not sufficient condition for exact causal order recovery.
  `exact_match` from `validation_suite.compare_causal_orders` is the criterion
  used here. The antichain case illustrates this: `final_energy = 4.757e-02 > 0`
  yet `exact_match = true` because all induced relations match the (empty) target.

## Next physical question (not yet executed)

The N=12 failure under historical schedule raises a diagnostic question:

> Is the failure of the historical annealer on N=12 (seed 1962) due to the
> specific temperature schedule (T₀=100, γ=0.9, 35 blocks), or does it reflect
> a more robust barrier in the energy landscape accessible under the historical
> move set and energy function?

Answering this requires a controlled comparison: running the same case
(`minkowski_12_s1962_d2_hist`) under a modified schedule — either tuned (Row E:
T₀=180, γ=0.8) or guarded warmup (Phase 2F variant) — while keeping the move set
and energy function fixed. That comparison has not been made and is not implied
by the results above.

If that comparison shows the tuned schedule recovers the N=12 case, the failure
is attributable to the schedule. If it does not, the barrier is more structural.
Either answer is a SORKIN-2 result, not a physics result about embeddability.
